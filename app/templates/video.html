Phishing Attacks: Definition, Red Flags, and Tips to Avoid Them


Definition:

A phishing attack is a type of cyber attack where the attacker tries to trick you into revealing confidential information, such as your passwords, credit card numbers, or social security numbers. They often do this by sending you an email or text message that looks like it's from a legitimate company, such as your bank or credit card company.

Red flags:

  • Unsolicited emails from unknown senders
  • Emails with typos or grammatical errors
  • Emails with links or attachments
  • Emails that ask for personal information

Tips:

  • Hover over links to see the real URL
  • Don't click on links or attachments in emails from unknown senders
  • Be careful about entering personal information online
